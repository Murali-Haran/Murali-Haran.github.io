Some data sources (there are many more on the web):

  • Access to Air Pollution Data: EPA AirData
    You can query the database to obtain information. For example, I created a small PA air quality data set (the latitudes and longitudes corresponding to county centroids were obtained from an online Census database).
  • Water Resources NSDI Node: link
  • NASA Atmospheric datasets on Southern U.S. and Central America ASA description of an old competition based on the dataset (and available data) Some data I created from it (with columns corresponding to longitude,latitude,data):
    • ozone (month 1) Dobson Units.
    • surface temperatures (month 1) Units: Kelvin (to convert to Celsius: degrees K - 273.15 = degrees C).
    • More details about the data are here .
  • The main NASA data website .
  • Wolfcamp aquifer data set (Cressie, 1993 pg.214): aquifer data . Piezometric-head data (in feet above sea level) at 85 distinct locations in the Wolfcamp aquifer of Texas and New Mexico.
