Spatial Models, spring 2010

Spatial Models (Stat 597A) is a graduate special topics course intended for students with a graduate level background in probability and statistical inference. Familiarity with basic stochastic processes and Monte Carlo methods (as covered in Stat 515) will also be very helpful. However, I may accomodate some advanced students from other departments who do not completely meet these requirements provided they contact me ahead of time.

Although the course will be focused on learning how to analyze spatial data, several topics covered are of more general interest and applicability, including Gaussian processes, Markov random field models, point processes, Bayesian inference and Monte Carlo/Markov chain Monte Carlo methods, and inference based on complex computer models. The discussion on Gaussian processes may be of particular interest to students interested in machine learning, nonparametric regression and classification. Post-docs and others who want to sit in on any part of the course are most welcome to contact me about doing so.

For more, see the detailed course announcement.
