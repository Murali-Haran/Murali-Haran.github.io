    Research

    I received my Ph.D. from the School of Statistics at the University of Minnesota and a B.S. in Computer Science from Carnegie Mellon University. My research interests include statistical computing (Markov chain Monte Carlo) and spatial modeling (primarily involving Bayesian hierarchical models), with applications to problems in ecology, geosciences, geography and epidemiology. I have also worked on statistical techniques for applications in software engineering. For more, please see: background and more on my research.
