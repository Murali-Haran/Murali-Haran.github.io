Tenure-Track Faculty Positions
Department of Statistics
The Pennsylvania State University

The Department of Statistics at Penn State seeks to fill multiple tenure-track/tenured positions, at any level, to begin August 2013. Screening will begin November 15, 2012.

In addition to its core strengths in likelihood theory, nonparametrics, computational methods, multivariate analysis, and other statistical topics, Penn State's Department of Statistics maintains numerous productive interdisciplinary relationships and boasts strong administrative support for nurturing these relationships. These interdisciplinary initiatives, each of which involves multiple statistics faculty members, include the Center for Astrostatistics , Comparative Genomics and Bioinformatics, Statistical Ecology and Environmental Statistics, Center for Infectious Disease Dynamics , and Statistical Genetics, along with the Methodology Center and the Statistical Consulting Center. In addition, multiple statistics faculty currently actively collaborate with faculty in the College of Information Sciences and Technology and the College of Earth and Mineral Sciences (specifically the world-renowned departments of meteorology, geosciences, and geology) at Penn State.

The Pennsylvania State University is located in the center of the state, in a valley surrounded by the Appalachian Mountains and state forest land. The adjoining town of State College is located within 250 miles of New York City, Washington, D.C., Philadelphia, Baltimore, and Pittsburgh. It offers excellent public schools, easy access to outdoor recreation, and many of the amenities of a metropolitan area—first-rate public transportation, world-class theater and concert events, advanced technology and research facilities—without the attending stress of urban life.

Information about Penn State's department of statistics can be found at www.stat.psu.edu; click the link to "Centers" for more details on the interdisciplinary initiatives listed above.

Basic Qualifications: Qualified applicants must demonstrate excellence in statistical research and teaching and have a Ph.D. in statistics or a related field completed or anticipated before August 2013.

Preferred Qualifications: All qualified candidates are encouraged to apply. Special consideration will be accorded to applicants who strengthen existing interdisciplinary ties of the Statistics Department (as listed above), or who have the potential to establish new ties. Examples include, but are not limited to, experts in machine learning, astrostatistics, or spatial statistics, or those who could be hired in conjunction with a special interdisciplinary initiative of the Huck Institutes of the Life Sciences at Penn State . We are also interested in applicants who have professional actuarial credentials and a desire to work with Penn State's actuarial program. In addition we are interested in candidates who are particularly well suited to the Penn State Cyberscience Cluster Hire in computation- and data- enabled science and engineering (see http://www.ics.psu.edu/hire.html).

Application Procedure: Please apply at mathjobs.org. Complete applications will consist of (1) a letter of application, (2) curriculum vitae, (3) three letters of recommendation, (4) a brief (1-2 page) teaching statement, (5) a brief (2-3 page) statement of research background and interests, and (6) a list of graduate-level courses taken in statistics. If you have any questions, please contact the search committee at facultysearch@stat.psu.edu

The Pennsylvania State University is committed to affirmative action, equal opportunity, and the diversity of its workforce. Employment will require successful completion of background check(s) in accordance with University policies.
